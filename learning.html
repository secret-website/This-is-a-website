BIO

KEVIN HUANG, PHD

PRINCIPAL INVESTIGATOR
Assistant Professor of Engineering
Department of Engineering
Trinity College, Hartford CT USA

Office: MECC 327
Lab: MECC 060

Kevin Huang graduated from Trinity College earning a BS with Honors in Mathematics and Honors in Engineering in 2012. He then earned his Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering at the Universtiy of Washington's BioRobotics Lab in 2017. That summer, Kevin started as Assistant Professor at Trinity College where he leads the PandA lab. He is also an Affiliate Assistant Professor with the Department of Electrical Engineering at the University of Washington. Kevin teaches several courses within the Trinity College Engineering curriculum:

  • ENGR 120 - Introduction to Engineering Design: Mobile Robots
  • ENGR 221 - Digital Circuits and Systems
  • ENGR 305 - Microelectronic Circuits
  • ENGR 312 - Automatic Control Systems

Daniel Melesse '20 I am Daniel Melesse from Bahir Dar, Ethiopia. I am currently double majoring in Electrical Engineering and Mathematics, and minoring in Models and Data. In the summer of my freshman year, I worked on an applied Math research under the guidance of Professor Ryan Pellico. My research title was "Synchronization in Nonlinear Harmonic Oscillator". Since the fall of 2017, I have been working on different research projects under the supervision of professor Kevin Haung in the PandA Lab. We worked in collaboration with the Psychology department to design a virtual reality vision software. Additionally, I have been working on research project titled "A Data Driven Approach for Automatic Gaze-Tracking". For this research we submitted a paper to the 14th IEEE International Conference on Signal Processing. My plan is to do a PhD in signal processing/machine learning.

His interest lies in the development of neural interface devices to better understand the human brain. While at Trinity, Prawesh conducted research within both Physics and Engineering Department starting his freshman year.
